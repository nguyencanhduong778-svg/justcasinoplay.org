4. Recognizing Problem Gambling

Gambling should be a form of entertainment, not a way to make money or escape problems. Some signs that gambling may be becoming a problem include:

  • Spending more money or time gambling than you can afford.
  • Chasing losses (trying to win back money you’ve lost).
  • Neglecting responsibilities at work, school, or home due to gambling.
  • Lying to family and friends about your gambling habits.
  • Feeling anxious, guilty, or depressed because of gambling.
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble.
  • Having frequent thoughts about gambling.
  • Experiencing urges to gamble when feeling upset, stressed, or bored.

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, it's important to seek help.
